Welcome to my homepage!

I’m a Postdoctoral Scholar at Texas State University. My research interests lie at the intersection of GIScience, spatial data science, human mobility, crime geography, and public health. I employ spatially explicit, theory-driven, and methodologically innovative approaches to tackle urgent social issues, including crime, mobility, and environmental vulnerability. By integrating advanced Geographic Information Systems (GIS) techniques with novel data sources, I develop rigorous tools for analyzing human behavior, social, and built environmnet in dynamic spatial settings.

Before this, I completed my Ph.D. in Geographic Information Science at the University of Cincinnati, where I investigated the impact of COVID-19 mobility reductions and facility closures on crime. I also hold master’s degrees in Geospatial Information Science and Criminal Justice from the University of Maryland.

I’ve led and contributed to multiple research, and have published in top journals like Applied Geography, Annals of AAG, CEUS, Habitat International, and Cities. I'm passionate about blending spatial computing and AI, urban analytics, and data visualization to solve real-world challenges.
