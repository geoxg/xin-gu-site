Projects

Extreme weather, natural disasters, and human movements

Investigating the impacts of global climate change and natural disasters on human mobility, with a focus on the dynamic mobility patterns and spatial accessibility of diverse social groups.

References:

Exploring Dynamic Human Mobility of Diverse Social Groups Under Extreme Heat Conditions: A Simulation-Based Approach (accepted, 2025)

Spatial Disparities in Fire Shelter Accessibility: Capacity Challenges in the Palisades and Eaton Fires (submitted, 2025)

Social Equity and Crime during COVID-19 Lockdowns

Exploring the impact of pandemic-related mobility restrictions and facility closures on urban crime spatial patterns and population equity through geospatial modeling..

References:

Measuring perceived racial heterogeneity and its impact on crime: An ambient population-based approach (Cities, 2023)

Examining the Impact of Mobility Reduction and Facility Closure during COVID-19 on Crime (OhioLINK Electronic Theses and Dissertations Center, 2024)

Capturing the Spatial Arrangement of POIs in Crime Modeling (CEUS, 2025)

The Impact of Changing Population Composition and Diversity on Crime during COVID-19 Lockdowns (Applied Geography, 2025)

Urban Infrastructure and Crime Dynamics

Examined the long-term spatial impacts of urban infrastructure and innovative transportation on crime distribution at a micro level in Cincinnati, OH.

References:

From Alleys to Arterial Streets: Unpacking the Influence of Diverse Street Types and Associated Business on Urban Theft Patterns (Applied Geography, 2025)

Assessing the impact of modern streetcar on street robberies: a longitudinal comparison in Cincinnati, OH (Habitat International, 2025)

Perceived Safety and Built Environment

Utilized social media, points-of-interest, and street view imagery data alongside multiscale models to evaluate how perceived safety, as well as social and built environment features, correlate with crime risks.
